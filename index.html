HexColored, an Award-Winning Mobile Puzzle Game

October 2016 - Current

Android Users! This game can be found here.

HexColored is a Puzzle Game built with the Unity game engine designed around placing hexes adjacent to one another, to clear like colors and combine colors according to the color wheel. This game currently has over 150 levels as of now. HexColored is available for download on the Google Play store. It will be released on the iOS App store in late 2018.

During the process of making this game, I designed and programmed the User Interface of the program, including the menu systems, button layout, color scheme, and all animations. I had also I also implemented a save functionality into the game. This was coded in C# scripts for Unity.

HexColored has won:

Best Puzzle Game - RIT ImagineCup Hackathon 2016

Outstanding UI - RPI Gamesfest 2017

Co-Up @ Magic Recipient - 2017

ImagineCup 2017 Semi-Finalist.

This was a group project and could not have been accomplished without my group members, John Palermo and Robert Bailey.

inDICEsion, a Family Board Game

August 2016 - October 2016

The Rules Sheet for this game can be found here.

inDICEsion [in-die-cision] is a dexterity game about throwing and rolling foam dice. Players must roll onto their boards in an attempt to score points equal to their roll, but can also throw their dice at opponent boards to knock down their opponent’s score.

During the process of making this board game, I worked on balancing the mechanics and abilities of the game, as to not create a dominant or game-breaking strategy. I also helped create artwork for the game, including the Boxart and Design Document, which can be found here.

This was a group project and could not have been accomplished without my group members, Robert Bailey, Noak Kellem, John Palermo, and Luke Hedrick.

Audio Visualizer

January 2017 - February 2017

The Audio Visualizer can be found here. (Desktop Only)

Note: The music will automatically start playing on this page!

My audio visualizer makes use of canvas artwork, all coded in Javascript. Using Anylyser nodes and Reverb nodes, the audio playing on the page is read into a data array and then effects the artwork displayed in the canvas.

I re-created the artwork from two Deadmau5 (c) albums to visualize the music playing on the page, and included the artwork of said albums alongside the canvas for reference. The artwork can also be modified to create a unique experience! Please note some of these modifications may result in lower performance.

The Original Mobile Games

June 2017 - Current

iOS Users! This game can be found here.
Android Users! This game can be found here.

The Original Mobile Games has been a co-production between RIT, The Strong National Museum of Play, and Second Avenue Learning. Pieces of history are brought back to life in this digital showcase of what were essentially the "original mobile games," including "ball in maze" games and a large variety of the sort, using gravity as their main mechanic. Download it for free today and start playing games from the late-1800s to the mid-1900s!

While this application has been in development since Summer of 2017, I had the honor of working on it from January to June of 2018. I was in charge of making vital game mecahnic updates, such as victory conditions, making sure sensativity was as accurate to their original counterparts as possible, along with UI updates such as programming the menu systems of the game, including their animations. I had also programmed and created game spaces for some of the games showcased, such as "Greased Pigs," "Race for the Pole," and "Beehive," to name a few. This project was developed in the Unity Game Engine and uses C# scripting.

Draw Together, a Multiplayer Browser Game

September 2017 - October 2017

Draw Together can be played here. (Desktop Only)

Draw Together is a Multiplayer game similar to Pictionary, where players draw images to the canvas based on given prompt. Their canvas is streamed to other players that will attempt guess the prompt to score points.

I coded this game using Node.js, along with socket.IO to allow multiplayer communication across browsers. Through this, the canvas image is drawn in real-time on other players' canvas element, simulating drawing on a piece of paper.

Adapterra, a Strategic Table-Top

April 2016 - May 2016

The Rules for Adapterra can be found here.

Adapterra is a TRPG (Tactical Role-playing game) board game in which players compete with one another in order to control areas of a remote planet. Through the use of nanobots and the ability to change to their environment, Adapterra offeres a wide variety of scenarios and play styles for many to enjoy.

During the process of making this board game, I worked on balancing the mechanics and abilities in the game, as to not create a dominant or game breaking strategy. I also created artwork for the game and background information and lore for which the game is based.

This was a group project and could not have been accomplished without my group members, Maurice Edwards, Christopher Brown, Alec Bielanos, Simon Voorhees, and Trevor Walden.
