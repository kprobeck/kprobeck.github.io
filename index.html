ColorCoded, an Award-Winning Mobile Puzzle Game

October 2016 - Current

The code for this project can be found here.
A preview for this project can be played here.

ColorCoded is a Puzzle Game designed around placing hexes adjacent to one another, to clear like colors and combine colors according to the color wheel. This game has over 100 levels as of now, and it is stil being developed, and will eventually be available for download on The Google Play and iOS App stores.

ColorCoded was entered in the 2017 RPI GameFest competition and was awarded for Outstanding User Interface.

ColorCoded was also submitted to the 2016 Microsoft Imagine Cup and made it to the SemiFinals.

During the process of making this game, I designed and programmed the User Interface of the program, including the menu systems, button layout, color scheme, and all animations. I had also programmed the mechanic that allows the player to pick up Hexes and place them into the puzzle, one of the main mechanics of the game. I also implemented a save functionality into the game, saving the player's process every time they complete a new level, and unlocking the next level for them to enjoy.

This was a group project and could not have been accomplished without my group members, John Palermo and Robert Bailey.

inDICEsion, a Family Board Game

August 2016 - October 2016

The Rules Sheet for this game can be found here.

inDICEsion [in-die-cision] is a dexterity game about throwing and rolling foam dice. Players must roll onto their boards in an attempt to score points equal to their roll, but can also throw their dice at opponent boards to knock down their opponent’s score.

During the process of making this board game, I worked on balancing the mechanics and abilities of the game, as to not create a dominant or game-breaking strategy. I also helped create artwork for the game, including the Boxart and Design Document, which can be found here.

This was a group project and could not have been accomplished without my group members, Robert Bailey, Noak Kellem, John Palermo, and Luke Hedrick.

Audio Visualizer

January 2017 - February 2017

The Audio Visualizer can be found here.

Note: The music will automatically start playing on this page, so please be aware of this!

My audio visualizer makes use of canvas artwork, all coded in Javascript. I re-created the artwork from two Deadmau5 (c) albums to visualize the music playing on the page, and included the artwork of said albums alongside the canvas for reference. Using Anylyser Nodes and Reverb nodes, the audio playing on the page is read into a data array and then effects the artwork displayed in the canvas.

The artwork can also be modified to create a unique experience! Please note some of these modifications may result in a lower performance and result in a lower framerate drawn on the canvas.

Adapterra, a Strategic Table-Top

April 2016 - may 2016

The Rules Sheet for this game can be found here.

Adapterra is a TRPG (Tactical Role-playing game) board game in which players compete with one another in order to control areas of a remote planet. Through the use of nanobots and the ability to change to their environment, Adapterra offeres a wide variety of scenarios and play styles for many to enjoy.

During the process of making this board game, I worked on balancing the mechanics and abilities in the game, as to not create a dominant or game breaking strategy. I also created artwork for the game and background information and lore for which the game is based.

This was a group project and could not have been accomplished without my group members, Maurice Edwards, Christopher Brown, Alec Bielanos, Simon Voorhees, and Trevor Walden.
